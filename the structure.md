Now, let's look at Pillar 2: The Pedagogical Structure.

The "problem" is that most students are trained to be "Memorizers." They expect to be asked what something is, not why it matters or what it assumes. If we hit them with "Analyze the logical fallacies in this History text" at Rank 1, they might feel like the game is too hard and quit.

We need a structure for Cognitive Scaffolding.

How do we "trick" the brain into thinking critically before it even realizes it's doing it?

Consider this structure:

Level 1: The "Difference Engine": Instead of definitions, we show two nearly identical statements and ask "Which one is more believable?" (Start of the RAVEN method).
Level 2: The "Why" Bounty: We take a standard WASSCE fact (e.g., "The water cycle causes rain") and put a "Bounty" on finding one thing that could break that cycle. This turns critical thinking into a "bug hunt."
Level 3: The "Socratic Duel": The student versus Clutch AI. The AI says something "wrong" on purpose, and the student wins XP by "calling out" the AI.
